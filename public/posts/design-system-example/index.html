Introduction#

Welcome to Colorful Cardboard, where Pokemon nostalgia meets serious investment strategy. This post demonstrates our complete design system, including typography, color usage, and content structure.

This is an example of a Market Analysis post, which uses our premium purple color scheme. Each category has its own visual identity to help you quickly find the content you’re looking for.

Typography Showcase#

This is an H3 Heading (Space Grotesk)#

Body text is set in Inter at 17px with a 1.7 line height for optimal readability. This size is larger than most blogs because we prioritize reading comfort for longer market analysis pieces.

Bold text stands out clearly for emphasis, while italic text can be used for card names or terms of art. Here’s an example: The Charizard VMAX from Darkness Ablaze remains a strong investment play.

This is an H4 Heading#

Smaller headings use reduced weight but maintain the Space Grotesk character. The hierarchy is clear and intentional.

Color System in Action#

Our five brand colors each serve a specific purpose:

  1. Electric Yellow (#FFD93D) - Beginner-friendly content, learning resources
  2. Deep Blue (#2B4C7E) - Primary brand color, main calls-to-action
  3. Cardinal Red (#DC2626) - Hot picks, time-sensitive opportunities
  4. Emerald Green (#059669) - Growth indicators, positive signals
  5. Royal Purple (#8B5CF6) - Premium analysis, rare insights

Each category uses these colors strategically. Since this is a Market Analysis post, you’ll see purple accents throughout, including the category badge at the top of this post.

Lists and Structure#

Unordered Lists#

Our investment thesis for 2026 includes:

  • Vintage sealed products continue to appreciate
  • Alt art cards from modern sets show strong demand
  • Graded PSA 10 premiums remain stable
  • Special sets (like 151 and Prismatic Evolutions) outperform
  • Eeveelution cards have consistent collector appeal

Ordered Lists#

Top 5 investment principles:

  1. Buy what you can afford to hold for 5-10 years minimum
  2. Diversify across eras (vintage, ex-era, modern)
  3. Prioritize condition - PSA 9-10 for modern, PSA 7-8 for vintage
  4. Follow the data - use price tracking tools
  5. Understand print runs - scarcity drives value

Responsive Design Notes#

This design system works beautifully across all devices:

  • Desktop (1024px+): Full two-column layout, sidebar table of contents
  • Tablet (768-1023px): Optimized spacing, touch-friendly targets
  • Mobile (<768px): Single column, reduced heading sizes, hamburger menu

The typography scales down appropriately, and touch targets remain a minimum of 44x44px for accessibility.

Dark Mode Support#

This entire design system works in dark mode! Toggle the theme switch in the header to see the transformation:

  • Background shifts to Slate 900
  • Text becomes Slate 100 (not harsh pure white)
  • Accent colors adjust for better contrast
  • Category colors maintain their identity
  • Shadows deepen for proper depth

Accessibility Features#

Every design decision prioritizes accessibility:

  • Color contrast: All text meets WCAG AA standards (most meet AAA)
  • Focus states: Visible keyboard navigation indicators
  • Semantic HTML: Proper heading hierarchy (h1 ‚Üí h2 ‚Üí h3)
  • Alt text: All images include descriptive alternatives
  • Font sizing: Base 17px for comfortable reading
  • Line spacing: 1.7 line-height reduces eye strain
